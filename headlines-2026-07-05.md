Fourth-of-July color pieces (Brooklyn Bridge fire, "America at 250" roundups), the July 4 speech, partisan-clash framing, and opinion columns were deliberately excluded in favor of durable policy, geopolitics, energy, and disaster-accountability stories.
- **NBC** — NBC's public Top Stories feed was heavily dominated by World Cup video clips (many from Telemundo, in Spanish), the Salute-to-America speech videos, and holiday-shopping posts, leaving thin same-day hard news. The five items above are the most significant hard-news stories in that feed within the window; each `nbcnews.com` link was confirmed live. Item 2 ("Millions pour into Tehran...") is carried in the NBC News feed but links to an NBC-family video hosted on `today.com`, the only same-day NBC-feed URL for that story. Item 5 (a mass shooting with eight victims, four of them children) is included as a mass-casualty event rather than routine crime-of-the-day.
- **AP** — `apnews.com` was reachable via direct `curl` but is not fetchable/searchable by the built-in web tools. AP was seeded via the Google News site-search feed specified for this digest, but current Google News RSS links use a client-side (JavaScript) redirect and do not resolve server-side to real article URLs. Each headline was therefore matched to its genuine `apnews.com/article/...` URL by fetching AP's homepage and section hubs, cross-verified against wire syndication (The Hill, Washington Post, Boston Globe, and others). No URLs were fabricated. Viral/novelty AP items (Brooklyn Bridge fire, Joey Chestnut, the Swift–Kelce wedding, a solo-rowing record) and crime/celebrity items (Paul Pelosi hit-and-run) were excluded.

Selection standard: everything above was chosen for likely significance 30 days out — war and energy chokepoints, defense and alliances, elections and governance, courts and civil-rights policy, public health, and natural disasters — over celebrity, crime-of-the-day, partisan theater, polling, sports, or viral novelty. Scheduled-event previews were deprioritized in favor of things that actually happened. All items were published within roughly 24 hours of 2026-07-05 13:35 GMT.
