Selection window: roughly 1:36 p.m. UTC July 15 through 1:36 p.m. UTC July 16, 2026. NBC's general feed (`feeds.nbcnews.com/nbcnews/public/news`) was dominated by World Cup semifinal video clips (Argentina–England), Telemundo Spanish-language sports segments, and shopping-deal content; to find substantive hard news, NBC's topic-specific feeds (politics, world, business, us-news, health) were also pulled directly (all 200) and cross-checked against the general feed. AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so it was located two ways: first via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`) to identify candidate stories and headlines, then verified and linked directly by fetching apnews.com's own homepage (200) and matching each story to its live, canonical `apnews.com/article/...` URL — avoiding any use of obfuscated `news.google.com` redirect links. Direct `curl` fetches of WSJ and NYT article pages (as opposed to their feeds) returned 401/403, which is expected subscriber-paywall/bot-blocking behavior for article pages; all five WSJ and five NYT links above are the exact URLs published in each outlet's own live RSS feed. All five NBC and five AP links were confirmed live with direct HTTP 200 responses. Selection prioritized throughlines independently corroborated across multiple outlets — Ukraine's ouster of Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov and the resulting protests, the growing split among House Democrats over military aid to Israel, the intensifying U.S.-Iran conflict (expanded strikes into northern Iran, a blockade-running ship disabled, diesel prices up 33% since the war began, and the release of a long-held U.S. citizen), a mass Rohingya migrant-boat tragedy in the Bay of Bengal, and Canadian wildfire smoke blanketing the U.S. Midwest and Northeast — alongside outlet-specific significant developments (a $100 billion TSMC investment in U.S. chipmaking, a new 25% U.S. tariff on Brazilian goods, FDA approval of a novel cholesterol drug, Texas flash flooding, the Attorney General confirmation hearing, and a new Pentagon testosterone-screening policy for troops), while excluding World Cup coverage, celebrity and obituary items (a "Jurassic Park" actor's cause of death, George Santos joining a reality show), crime-of-the-day stories, and other novelty- or virality-driven items.
