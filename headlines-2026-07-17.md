## Note

Selection window: roughly 1:38 p.m. UTC July 16 through 1:38 p.m. UTC July 17, 2026. WSJ was fetched via its public Dow Jones RSS feeds (World News, US Business, US News, Politics, Economy, Tech, Arts & Culture, Health) with a browser-style User-Agent; the World and Business feeds carry clean headlines, while the Politics/Economy social feeds publish full teaser sentences as their `<title>` field, so those two links' display headlines were reconstructed from the article's URL slug rather than quoted from the feed's raw title text. NYT was fetched via its public Top Stories RSS feed. NBC was fetched via `feeds.nbcnews.com/nbcnews/public/news`; its general feed leans heavily on video clips and shopping/lifestyle content (skincare deals, product roundups), which were excluded in favor of the hard-news items present in the same feed — as a result two of the five NBC links point to video pages rather than text articles, which is the outlet's own framing of those stories. AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so candidate stories were located via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`) as instructed; the `news.google.com` redirect links in that feed use an obfuscated format that does not resolve via a simple HTTP redirect or base64 decode, so instead each story was cross-referenced against apnews.com's own topic-hub pages (Iran, Britain, Israel, financial markets, artificial intelligence) to find the matching canonical `apnews.com/article/...` URL, which was then confirmed live and checked against its `article:published_time` metadata to verify it fell inside the 24-hour window. Selection prioritized the throughline that recurred independently across all four outlets — the U.S. campaign of airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure (bridges and a port near the Strait of Hormuz) and Andy Burnham's confirmation as the U.K.'s incoming prime minister — alongside outlet-specific significant developments (a historic 25% stock plunge at IBM and broader Wall Street anxiety over AI spending, Xi Jinping's push to shape global AI governance while criticizing U.S. tech restrictions, the substance and institutional stakes of Trump's prime-time speech alleging election interference, Israel's Knesset dissolving ahead of an October election, a $95 billion congressional Iran-war funding package, Ukraine's defense-minister shake-up, deadly Texas flash flooding, a Kremlin crackdown on a dissident blogger, and hazardous wildfire-smoke air quality across the Midwest and East Coast), while excluding the Taco Bell lettuce outbreak, ICE-shooting human-interest coverage, sports (British Open, World Cup fallout, MLB scheduling), celebrity/shopping content, and other novelty- or outrage-driven items.
