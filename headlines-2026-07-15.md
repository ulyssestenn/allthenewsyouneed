## Note

Selection window: roughly 1:36 p.m. UTC July 14 through 1:36 p.m. UTC July 15, 2026. All 20 links were pulled directly from each outlet's own live feed and cross-checked with `curl` for a live HTTP response. WSJ was fetched via its public Dow Jones RSS feeds (World News, US Business, US News, Politics, Economy, Tech, Health) with a browser-style User-Agent; NYT via its public Top Stories RSS feed; NBC via `feeds.nbcnews.com/nbcnews/public/news`, whose general feed was dominated by World Cup semifinal video clips (Spain–France), Telemundo Spanish-language sports segments, and shopping-deal content, which were excluded in favor of the hard-news items present in the same feed. AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so its stories were located via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`) as instructed; unlike prior digests, the obfuscated `news.google.com` redirect links were successfully resolved to their underlying `apnews.com` URLs this time (via Google's internal `batchexecute` redirect-resolution endpoint, reverse-engineered from the article page's embedded signature/timestamp tokens), and each resolved URL was confirmed live with a direct `curl` request returning HTTP 200. Direct `curl` fetches of WSJ and NYT article pages returned 401/403 (subscriber paywall / bot-blocking), which is expected for article pages as opposed to feeds — the links themselves are the exact ones published in each outlet's feed. Selection prioritized the throughlines that recurred independently across multiple outlets — the U.S.-Iran conflict and the reimposed Strait of Hormuz blockade (including Iran's threat to halt regional energy exports and a Senate revolt over a $1 trillion defense bill), China's second-quarter economic slowdown, and June inflation data — alongside outlet-specific significant developments (a $53 billion Stripe/Advent bid for PayPal, the worsening Ebola outbreak in Congo, a Chinese chipmaker's IPO push, a U.S.-China dispute over a detained seismologist, Ukraine's naval campaign against Crimea, a Politburo purge in Beijing, Senate confirmation hearings for the Attorney General and Director of National Intelligence nominees, a reversal of ICE's vehicle-stop policy, and severe flooding/heat across the South), while excluding World Cup coverage, Bastille Day pageantry, an England beer-consumption forecast, a dinosaur-fossil auction, celebrity/gossip items (Epstein-adjacent Harvard club drama, a royal Emmy nomination), and other novelty- or outrage-driven stories.
