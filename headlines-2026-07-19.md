## Note

Selection window: roughly 1:36 p.m. UTC July 18 through 1:36 p.m. UTC July 19, 2026. WSJ was fetched via its public Dow Jones RSS feeds (World News, US Business, WSJD/Tech, US News, Politics, Economy, Arts & Culture, Health) with a browser-style User-Agent. NYT was fetched via its public World, US, Business, and Politics section RSS feeds (the general Top Stories feed was thin on hard news today, so the section feeds were pulled to find enough material within the window). Both WSJ and NYT article pages return bot-protection HTTP status codes (401/403) to automated requests without a browser session, so the links here could not be live-verified by fetching the article page directly; instead they are the exact, unmodified `<link>` values published in each outlet's own official RSS feed, which is the authoritative source for the URL and headline text.

NBC's public RSS feeds (news, world, politics, business, US news) were unusually thin on hard news in this window — the general feed was dominated almost entirely by World Cup final coverage (largely in Spanish, via Telemundo) plus shopping/lifestyle content, which were excluded per the selection standard. As a result, two of NBC's five links point to a video page and a sports-business item (a broadcast-rights deal) rather than harder text-news stories, and one item (the Forest Service hostage situation) is a genuine but comparatively minor domestic safety story included to round out the section, since no other qualifying hard-news item was found in NBC's own feeds within the 24-hour window. This mirrors a known limitation of NBC's public feed noted on prior days.

AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so candidate stories were located via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`) as instructed. The `news.google.com` redirect links in that feed use an obfuscated format that does not resolve via a simple HTTP redirect, so each candidate headline was instead cross-referenced against apnews.com's own topic-hub pages (Iran, Russia-Ukraine, financial markets, Hungary) to find the matching canonical `apnews.com/article/...` URL, which was then fetched directly and confirmed live via its `og:title` and `article:published_time` metadata to verify both the exact headline text and that it fell inside the 24-hour window. One AP candidate ("The sell-off for AI stars worsens, while oil prices keep jumping," surfaced by Google News on July 18) was excluded after its `article:published_time` metadata showed an original publish date of July 17, outside the window.

Selection prioritized the U.S.-Iran war's escalation into direct American casualties (two U.S. service members killed and one missing in an Iranian strike on Jordan, bringing the American death toll to 16, and the U.S. military's retaliatory strikes) and a major Russia-Ukraine escalation (Russia's large-scale missile assault on Kyiv and Ukraine's retaliatory drone strikes on Russian warehouses/logistics sites), which recurred independently across all four outlets, alongside outlet-specific developments of durable significance: Netanyahu's narrowing political position amid the war, the U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve falling to its lowest level since 1983, a Justice Department pullback on corporate-crime prosecutions, the Trump administration's rollback of federal civil-rights enforcement, a Chicago federal prosecutor's office reeling from collapsing immigration cases, unrest within Ukraine's military leadership, and Hungary's president signing a constitutional amendment ending his own term as part of a broader political realignment. Excluded were the World Cup final and related sports coverage, the Tate brothers' arrest, a Rhode Island fugitive's capture, the Cyclospora lettuce recall's consumer-shopping angle, and other novelty-, celebrity-, or virality-driven items.
