## Note

Selection window: roughly 1:30 p.m. UTC July 19 through 1:30 p.m. UTC July 20, 2026. WSJ was fetched via its public Dow Jones RSS feeds (World News, US Business, WSJD/Tech, US News, Politics, Economy, Arts & Culture, Health) with a browser-style User-Agent, as in prior days. NYT was fetched via its public Top Stories/HomePage RSS feed, which was well-stocked with hard news today. NBC's general public feed was again dominated almost entirely by World Cup-final aftermath (largely Spanish-language Telemundo video content), so, as on prior days, its section feeds (world, politics, business) were also pulled to surface enough hard news within the window; two of NBC's five links are consequently to NBC/Today video pages rather than text articles, since no text-article equivalent of those particular breaking updates existed in NBC's own feeds at fetch time. WSJ and NYT article pages return bot-protection HTTP status codes to automated requests without a browser session, so those links could not be live-verified by fetching the article page directly; they are the exact, unmodified `<link>` values published in each outlet's own official RSS feed.

AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so candidate stories were located via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`). As on prior days, the `news.google.com` redirect links in that feed do not resolve via a simple HTTP redirect, so each candidate headline was cross-referenced against apnews.com's own topic-hub pages (Iran, UK, Russia-Ukraine, immigration/DOJ) to find the matching canonical `apnews.com/article/...` URL, which was then fetched directly and confirmed live via its `og:title` and `article:published_time` metadata to verify both exact headline text and that it fell inside the 24-hour window.

Selection prioritized the U.S.-Iran war's continued escalation — additional American troop deaths in Jordan and Iraq (the Pentagon identifying two soldiers killed in Jordan and disclosing it had withheld dozens of injury reports), expanding U.S. airstrikes on Iran, Iran's retaliation against Gulf states, and the resulting spike in oil and gasoline prices — which recurred independently across all four outlets, alongside Andy Burnham's formal accession as the UK's new prime minister (a genuine transfer of power, not a scheduled-event preview) and Ukraine's Zelensky facing internal political pressure over his military leadership shake-up. Rounding out the outlet-specific picks were durable-significance stories not tied to the two dominant threads: a hunger-strike-driven youth protest movement against India's Modi government, and the Justice Department's activation of a new deportation court. Excluded were the World Cup final and all related sports/celebration coverage, the Tate brothers' arrest, the Usha Vance birth announcement, the Lindsay Clancy murder trial, the Utah flash-flood deaths, the Peru earthquake, the Guyana ferry capsizing, and other novelty-, tragedy-of-the-day-, or celebrity-driven items whose significance does not extend much past the news cycle.
