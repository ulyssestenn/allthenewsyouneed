## Note

Selection window: roughly 1:36 p.m. UTC July 17 through 1:36 p.m. UTC July 18, 2026. WSJ was fetched via its public Dow Jones RSS feeds (World News, US Business, US News, Politics, Economy, Tech, Arts & Culture, Health) with a browser-style User-Agent; each headline above is a verbatim `<title>` from a clean-headline feed (World, Tech, Politics) rather than one of the teaser-sentence feeds. NYT was fetched via its public Top Stories RSS feed, which this cycle skewed heavily toward opinion, lifestyle, and soft-culture items (a Syracuse-writers real-estate piece, testosterone-themed opinion column, a briefing newsletter roundup) with little hard news beyond what's listed; the fifth slot went to the Trump-Meloni ambassador story as the most substantive remaining item, even though its "superyacht diplomacy" framing is lighter than the other four. NBC was fetched via `feeds.nbcnews.com/nbcnews/public/news`; as on prior days its general feed leans heavily on shopping/lifestyle content (a Nordstrom sale, beach gear, tick repellent) and video clips, which were excluded in favor of hard-news items in the same feed, and several crime/human-interest stories (a hostage rescue, Paul Pelosi's hit-and-run charge, an ICE-shooting wake) were deliberately passed over as outrage- or novelty-driven under the selection standard. AP does not publish a public RSS feed, so candidate stories were located via Google News's site-restricted search (`site:apnews.com when:1d`) as instructed; that feed's `news.google.com` redirect links do not resolve to canonical `apnews.com` URLs via a simple HTTP redirect (confirmed with `curl -w %{redirect_url}`, which just echoes the same obfuscated Google URL back), so each story was instead cross-referenced against AP's own topic-hub pages (Iran, wildfires, artificial intelligence, financial markets, elections, Donald Trump) to find the matching canonical `apnews.com/article/...` URL, which was then fetched live and checked against its `datePublished` metadata to confirm it fell inside the 24-hour window. Selection prioritized the two throughlines that recurred independently across all four outlets — the escalating U.S.-Iran exchange of strikes around the Strait of Hormuz, and Canadian wildfire smoke blanketing the Midwest, Northeast, and D.C. (affecting an estimated 100 million Americans) — alongside outlet-specific significant developments (a SpaceX-Pentagon data-center deal, a growing threat to Saudi Arabia's main oil-export route, the newly public election-intelligence files behind Trump's primetime election-fraud speech and Sen. Mullin's follow-up pressure on states, a Russia sanctions bill raising dollar-related concerns, Israel's counter-espionage push against Iranian recruitment, the U.S. letting its Hong Kong emergency declaration lapse, and a Chinese AI lab's Kimi K3 model release rattling U.S. tech stocks), while excluding the World Cup final, British Open golf, the ongoing Taco Bell/Taylor Farms lettuce recall, ICE-shooting and hit-and-run human-interest coverage, campaign horse-race items (Nebraska's Senate race, Wisconsin's governor field), a damage-free Mexico-Guatemala earthquake, and opinion/celebrity/shopping content.
